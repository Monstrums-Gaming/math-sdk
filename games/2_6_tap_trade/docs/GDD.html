1Overview

Tap Trade is a continuous, tap-to-bet prediction game (the “Euphoria” mechanic). A live-looking price chart scrolls forever; the future region of the chart is covered by a grid of (time × price) multiplier cells. The player taps a cell to place a chip. If the price line reaches that cell, the chip pays bet × cellMultiplier; otherwise it loses. There are no rounds and no timers — each chip resolves the moment the line reaches its column.

The one-sentence pitch: the thrill of calling a price move, priced like a certified casino game — every cell on screen is a real published bet mode, and the chart is steered theatre over pre-generated, hash-frozen outcomes.

2Core Gameplay

2.1 The loop

  1. Tap a future cell. Each cell displays a multiplier snapped to the published ladder. A chip is placed for the current bet size (one real /wallet/play at that cell’s mode).
  2. The line approaches. The chip pulses pending → active → hot (with a depleting countdown bar) as its column nears the now-line.
  3. Reveal. The line is steered to hit (win) or miss (lose) the cell per the drawn book. A win fills the cell gold with a ring pulse and pays stake × M; a loss flashes red, shakes, and drops away.

2.2 Grid geometry

Cell size5 s wide × $0.50 tall (14 px/s × 110 px/$ at zoom 1)
Now-lineAt 40% of the canvas width; history scrolls left of it
Dead zoneCells starting < 2.5 s ahead cannot be bet (shaded)
Reveal windowA chip resolves within 1.6 s of its column reaching the now-line
Zoom0.5×–2.5× (wheel / pinch / buttons) — rescales pixels only, never odds

2.3 Placement rules

  • Cell must start ≥ 2.5 s in the future (“Too close to the line”).
  • One chip per cell (“Chip already there”).
  • Max two chips per time-column, on distinct cells (“Column full”).
  • Stake must be ≤ the displayed balance (queued chips reserve funds).
  • Chips in other columns are unrestricted — their rounds are serialized through the bet queue, not rejected.
  • Tap-to-confirm (setting; default on for touch): first tap previews the cell and its exact offer, second tap places.

Why exactly two chips per column. During a column’s reveal window the line sweeps one contiguous band of prices, so the column’s winners must be adjacent in price with every loser outside the band. With 2 chips all 4 outcome patterns are drawable (sweep both, hit either, thread between). With 3 chips a win-lose-win sandwich (1 of 8 draws) is impossible to render; with 4 chips ~31% of patterns are. Payouts would still be correct — but the line would visibly contradict a result. The cap is static so a rejection never leaks anything about outcomes already drawn on the board.

3Game Math

3.1 Model

Tap Trade is a direct-probability game: no board, no reels, no free spins, Rust optimiser off. Every distinct cell multiplier is its own published bet mode call_<cents> (cost 1.0). Lookup-table weights are uniform (1 per book), so published odds equal book counts exactly — there is no approximation anywhere in the pipeline.

3.2 Ladder derivation

For each multiplier M, the win probability is the smallest-denominator rational a/b whose realised RTP (a/b) × M lands in [96.00%, 96.70%] (the _simplest_fraction_in Stern-Brocot descent shared with Limbo/Chicken). num_sims = b, so a build yields exactly a winning books out of b — the LUT is the odds.

Why the floor is 1.4×, not 1.2×: a 1.2× win/lose mode has payout standard deviation ≈ 0.48, below ACP’s Base-Volatility floor of 0.60 — which rates the whole game off its tamest mode. 1.4× clears it (~0.62). The 100× ceiling is the approved Limbo base_100 precedent; the whole ladder sits inside that envelope at both ends. Every mode is a two-outcome all-or-nothing bet.

3.4 The per-mode wincap quirk (intentional)

Each mode’s BetMode.max_win equals its own multiplier M (not the global 100×). The engine applies a per-mode wincap override during sims, so a winning book — whose payout equals M — always reaches the active mode’s cap and emits a standard wincap event. Every winning book in every mode is therefore cellCall → wincap → finalWin; a losing book is cellCall → finalWin. The web client treats wincap as an explicit no-op. Do not “fix” this.

3.5 Display pricing vs true odds

The multiplier a cell shows comes from a local volatility model (normal-CDF band probability × 1.55, house-edge 3.5%) snapped in log-space to the nearest ladder rung — so every offered value is a real published mode. This model is presentation only: the true odds always come from the mode the chip plays.

5Fairness & Compliance

5.1 Certified replay

The Stake Engine RGS is a certified replay system: books and lookup tables are pre-generated, hash-verified, and frozen at publish time. Odds cannot change at runtime. The platform owns the provably-fair seed pair; its HMAC selects which pre-generated book a round serves.

5.2 Position-neutral books

The book encodes only win/lose and the offered multiplier. Where the tapped cell sits is pure client-side presentation — the chart is steered to hit or miss the player’s cell from the drawn outcome. Prices are RNG theatre; money is book-driven.

5.3 ACP rules satisfied

  • 0.1× LUT grid — every multiplier is a multiple of 0.10.
  • Per-mode RTP within [90%, 96.70%] — realised 96.00–96.55%.
  • Cross-mode spread ≤ 1.00% — realised 0.5517%.
  • Base bet mode cost = 1.0 (all 28 modes).
  • Risk/star-rating: all-or-nothing two-outcome modes inside the approved Limbo 1.40×–100× envelope (the one gate not provable locally).

5.4 Regulated-market behavior

jurisdiction.disabledTurboHides the 1.5×/2× speeds; resets a persisted fast speed to 1×

Demo gatingLOCAL play-money runs only in dev builds or behind ?demo=1; a paramless production launch shows a blocking “launch from the casino” overlay

6Technical Architecture

6.1 Two repositories, one contract

  run.py → create_books → generate_configs → execute_all_tests
  build_odds_bundle.py → library/odds_bundle.json   # re-verifies artifacts; feeds the client ladder

  src/game/…  src/components/…            # consumes books via the shared actor → handler pipeline

6.2 The book contract (verified against published books)

win:   cellCall {result:"Win",  isWin:true,  payoutMultiplier:<M×100>, winChance:a/b}
       → wincap  {amount:<M×100>}          # every winning book — client no-op
       → finalWin{amount:<M×100>}
lose:  cellCall {result:"Lose", isWin:false, payoutMultiplier:<M×100>, winChance:a/b}
       → finalWin{amount:0}

Mode keys are call_<multiplierCents>; the client ladder derives from odds_bundle.json so math naming wins.

6.3 Money scales (three different ones — the classic trap)

FieldScaleExample ($5 bet at 4.5×)

6.4 Round lifecycle

/wallet/authenticate → balance, bet levels, jurisdiction, resumable round · /wallet/play (mode, ×1e6 amount) → book events under round.state · a loss settles inside the play response (round.active:false); only a win calls /wallet/end-round (the shared machine’s singleRoundWin path calls it eagerly and applies the balance after the play phase). One active round per session.

6.5 Multi-chip over a one-round machine: the tap queue

The shared xstate actor accepts BET only in idle. Taps therefore queue app-side (game/tapQueue.svelte.ts) and pump serially: each chip sets its own activeBetModeKey/betAmount just before dispatch; the cellCall handler does not await the reveal animation (opposite of crypto-pulse), so a round settles in ~1 RTT and the queue drains while the line is still travelling.

6.6 The masked balance ledger

Server balances (including a win’s end-round credit) land at settlement, before the line reaches the chip. The HUD therefore reads a display ledger: place = −stake · win reveal = +payout · loss = no change, and reconciles to the authoritative stateBet.balanceAmount only when nothing is queued, in flight, or unrevealed. Per-round balance snapshots must never drive the pill — out-of-order reveals drift it by whole stakes (the “double-deduction” bug class).

6.7 Scene engine & steering

Raw 2D canvas, one rAF loop, fixed-tick accumulator (tick 0.1 s × timeScale 1.3 × speed setting; hidden-tab backlog is dropped so chips never resolve off-screen). Steering per nearest unresolved column: winners — converge on the closest unrevealed winner, then sweep to the other; losers — hold a miss lane clear of every losing band; inside the reveal window the outcome is hard-guaranteed with a margin clamp so a losing cell is never touched.

Svelte 5 lesson (hard-won): object references fork across $state proxy trees. A chip (tapState) holding a reference to its history entry (historyState) ended up mutating a different object than the panel rendered. Cross-store links must be by id, resolved through the owning store. Also: never key an {#each} by Date.now() — two chips in one column resolve in the same sim tick.

6.8 Offline & debug

mockRgs.ts monkey-patches fetch (dev or ?demo=1 only) with live-faithful settlement — a win stays active until end-round, so the real machine path runs offline. Debug handles (dev builds only): window.__tt (sim time, cfg, tapState), window.__ttHistory; the demo exposes window.__cpg.
